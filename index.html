Sunset Park Community Organization Working to Make Indigenous Voices Count

Sept. 24, 2020

By Sophia Lebowitz

Mixteca Organization, serving Sunset Park’s Latinx community, has been doing census and COVID-19 outreach in four different Indigenous Mesoamerican languages: Tu’u Savi, Ki’iche, Nahuatl, and Mixe, which are all spoken in Sunset Park. In-person census outreach was derailed by the COVID-19 shutdown. Mixteca held events almost every day in Sept. to make up for lost time. At these events Indigenous speakers were present to engage with the portion of Sunset Park’s Mexican immigrant community that is not comfortable with Spanish. As of 2018, New York State Assembly District 51, which includes Sunset Park, was almost 25% immigrants of Central American origin according to data from Social Explorer. Dr. Daniel Kaufman, the founder of the Endangered Language Alliance, said that recently “the major sending zone from Mexico for immigrants to New York expanded from Puebla into Guerrero. Guerrero is really one of the most neglected rural states in Mexico. And the majority, especially from the sending region, are Indigenous and speakers of an Indigenous language.”

According to INEGI, about 90% of migrants from the states of Puebla and Guerrero, end up in the United States. These states have a large population of indigenous speakers.

Around 80% of households in Sunset Park are considered "hard to count" by the U.S. Census Bureau.

According to the U.S. Census Bureau there are over 15,000 people of Hispanic/Latino origin in New York City that speak a language other than Spanish and English.
